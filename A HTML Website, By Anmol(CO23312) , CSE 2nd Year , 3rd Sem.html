CCET(DG):

on-campus beverages you should be drinking

Rupali

Last Updated Feb 6th, 2018

  1. Italian soda at Bytes

    2. Near Block A, CCET:DG, Sector 26

    When's the last time you've had an Italian soda? It had been a while for me, but I wanted to join my friend for a non-caffeinated beverage at Bytes and I became reacquainted with the combination of fizzy water and artificial fruit syrup. Italian Sodas are not so sweet as a normal soda and not so boring as a flavoured carbonated water. The Italian sodas at bytes also have cream in them (or maybe they do if you ask; I haven't tried), which makes it a nice coffee shop alternative for the times when you are feeling a refreshing drink that doesn't have caffeine or milk.

    Recommendation: Raspberry Italian soda

  2. Bubble tea at Pop Tea Bar

    3. Near Block A, CCET:DG, Sector 26

    Notice the carefully selected interior? The factors involved are a mixture of modern design and classic old school comfort. The bubble tea options here are diverse, catering to both first-time drinkers and bubble tea enthusiasts. What stands out about Pop Tea Bar is their commitment to quality ingredients - the tea is freshly brewed, the milk is high quality, and the tapioca pearls are perfectly cooked to give that chewy texture. Their menu includes an array of flavors such as classic milk tea, taro, matcha, and fruit teas. Each drink can be customized with different levels of sweetness and ice, and you can add various toppings like jelly, pudding, or even extra tapioca pearls. Pop Tea Bar is more than just a place to get your bubble tea fix; it's a community spot where students gather to study, chat, and enjoy a drink that's both fun and refreshing.

    Recommendation: Taro Bubble Tea

  3. Latte at CoHo

    4. Near Block A, CCET:DG, Sector 26

    If you need a break from your studies, CoHo is the perfect place to relax with a delicious latte. Their lattes are made with freshly brewed espresso and steamed milk, creating a rich and creamy beverage that's hard to resist. Whether you prefer it hot or iced, the latte at CoHo is sure to give you the caffeine boost you need to power through your day. The ambiance at CoHo adds to the experience, with cozy seating areas, plenty of natural light, and a menu that includes a variety of pastries and snacks to accompany your drink. Regulars at CoHo appreciate the consistency in quality and the friendly baristas who remember their orders. It's a place where you can feel at home, whether you're catching up with friends or diving into a good book. The latte here isn't just a drink; it's a comforting ritual that makes your day a little brighter.

    Recommendation: Vanilla Latte

  4. Agua Fresca at Nexus Cafe

    5. Near Block A, CCET:DG, Sector 26

    For a refreshing and hydrating drink, try the Agua Fresca at Nexus Cafe. This traditional Mexican beverage is made with fresh fruit, water, and a touch of sugar, making it the perfect drink to cool down on a hot day. With flavors like watermelon, mango, and pineapple, there's an Agua Fresca for everyone to enjoy. The Nexus Cafe prides itself on using only the freshest ingredients, ensuring that each drink is bursting with natural flavor. The vibrant colors of the Agua Fresca are as appealing to the eyes as the taste is to the palate. Whether youâ€™re sitting outside enjoying the weather or taking a break from the bustling campus life inside, an Agua Fresca from Nexus Cafe is a rejuvenating treat. The cafe also offers a range of healthy snacks and light meals, making it a great spot for a nutritious and delicious break in your day.

    Recommendation: Watermelon Agua Fresca
